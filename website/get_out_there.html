Get Out There

What is it?

An important part of curbing climate change is physically going out and trying to fix what we have caused. Whether it is by volunteering to plant trees, picking up trash, or so much more we are able to make a change. Getting out there is great if you want to see what your actions can do right away. There is nothing like seeing the trees you planted grow, or looking at a beach knowing that you have helped to keep it clean.

Ideas

Plant Trees

Planting trees is always highlighted as an easy way to help the environment because of how it can take carbon out of the atmosphere. While talking about curbing climate change you may think that planting trees will not have a big enough effect. If we as a society work together to plant at least 1 trillion trees, then we can cut atmospheric carbon by about 25%(Geggel) which is instrumental.

Clean the Beach

The oceans are able to keep "0-50% of carbon dioxide emissions from human-related activities"out of the atmosphere and therefore preventing catastrophic levels of global warming. However phytoplankton which photosynthesize the carbon have been consuming broken down plastics which causes their ability to break down carbon to leason. Therefore it is detrimental that we stop the plastics from entering the ocean in the first place, as well as clean up what is already in the ocean. Every little piece of trash taken out can help us lead to a clean ocean and better functioning phytoplankton.(Bauman)
